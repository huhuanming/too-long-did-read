# Anthropic 教育报告：教育工作者如何使用 Claude
# Anthropic Education Report: How Educators Use Claude

> 原文链接: https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-education-report-how-educators-use-claude
> 日期: 2025-08-27

---

## English

This comprehensive analysis examines how university educators integrate Claude AI into their professional work. Anthropic analyzed approximately 74,000 anonymized conversations from higher education professionals during May and June 2025, complemented by qualitative research from 22 Northeastern University faculty members.

### Key Findings

**Primary Use Cases:** Curriculum development dominates educator usage at 57% of conversations analyzed. Academic research and student performance assessment follow as secondary applications. Educators also dedicate significant time to professional development, with surveyed faculty allocating roughly 29% of their AI usage to personal learning.

**Tool Development:** Rather than limiting themselves to conversation, professors leverage Claude Artifacts to build functional educational resources. These creations span interactive chemistry simulations, automated grading rubrics, data visualization dashboards, educational games with gamification elements, and assessment tools with automatic feedback systems.

**Augmentation vs. Automation Patterns:** Educators demonstrate clear preferences based on task type. Activities involving direct student interaction—such as lesson design, advising, and grant writing—favor collaborative approaches (67-77% augmentation). Administrative and financial tasks show higher automation tendencies (44-65% automation), though grading presents a concerning exception with 48.9% automation despite educators rating it as AI's least effective application.

### Notable Tensions

**Grading Concerns:** A significant disconnect exists between what the data shows and educator sentiment. While nearly half of grading-related interactions involved full automation, surveyed faculty considered this the weakest AI application. One professor expressed ethical reservations, noting that "students are not paying tuition for the LLM's time, they're paying for my time."

**Curricular Adaptation:** Recognizing that AI availability transforms student learning, educators are fundamentally redesigning assessments. Some abandon traditional research papers entirely, instead crafting assignments that demand higher-order thinking and resist AI completion. This shift represents both opportunity and burden, as maintaining relevance requires constant vigilance against technological advances.

### Methodological Limitations

The analysis captures only conversations explicitly linked to educational tasks, likely missing broader educator engagement with AI. The study focuses exclusively on higher education email accounts, excluding K-12 educators. Early-adopter bias means findings may not represent the wider educator population's technological comfort or perspectives.

## 中文翻译

这项全面分析考察了大学教育工作者如何将 Claude AI 整合到他们的专业工作中。Anthropic 分析了 2025 年 5 月和 6 月期间来自高等教育专业人士的约 74,000 个匿名对话，并辅以来自 22 位东北大学教职人员的定性研究。

### 关键发现

**主要使用场景：** 课程开发在分析的对话中占 57%，主导了教育工作者的使用。学术研究和学生表现评估是次要应用。教育工作者还将大量时间用于专业发展，受访教职人员将约 29% 的 AI 使用分配给个人学习。

**工具开发：** 教授们不仅限于对话，还利用 Claude Artifacts 构建功能性教育资源。这些创作涵盖交互式化学模拟、自动评分标准、数据可视化仪表板、带游戏化元素的教育游戏以及具有自动反馈系统的评估工具。

**增强与自动化模式：** 教育工作者根据任务类型表现出明确的偏好。涉及直接学生互动的活动——如课程设计、咨询和撰写基金申请——倾向于协作方式（67-77% 增强）。行政和财务任务显示出更高的自动化倾向（44-65% 自动化），但评分是一个值得关注的例外，虽然教育工作者将其评为 AI 效果最差的应用，自动化比例却达到了 48.9%。

### 值得注意的矛盾

**评分问题：** 数据显示的内容与教育工作者的情感之间存在显著脱节。虽然近一半与评分相关的互动涉及完全自动化，但受访教职人员认为这是 AI 最薄弱的应用。一位教授表达了伦理上的保留意见，指出"学生交学费不是为了 LLM 的时间，而是为了我的时间。"

**课程调整：** 认识到 AI 的可用性改变了学生学习方式，教育工作者正在从根本上重新设计评估方式。一些人完全放弃了传统的研究论文，转而设计需要高阶思维且不易被 AI 完成的作业。这种转变既是机遇也是负担，因为保持相关性需要对技术进步保持持续警觉。

### 方法论局限

该分析仅捕获了明确与教育任务相关的对话，可能遗漏了教育工作者更广泛的 AI 使用。研究仅关注高等教育电子邮件账户，不包括 K-12 教育工作者。早期采用者偏差意味着研究发现可能不代表更广泛教育工作者群体的技术适应度或观点。
